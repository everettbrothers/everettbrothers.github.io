  ANALYTICS SETUP — pick ONE, takes ~2 minutes
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Your three HTML pages already have both options built in as commented-out
code near the bottom of each <head>. You just sign up, get one line/ID,
paste it in, and uncomment it. Do this in all 3 files (index, work, contact).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OPTION 1 — PLAUSIBLE  (recommended: simple, private, no cookie banner)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Go to plausible.io and create an account (paid, ~$9/mo — but has a
   30-day free trial, and it's the cleanest option).
   OR use a free alternative: "Umami" (umami.is) or "Counter.dev" — both free.
2. Add your site: everett-brothers.com
3. They give you a <script> line. In each HTML file, find the
   "OPTION 1: Plausible" comment and replace the commented line with
   theirs, removing the <!-- and --> around it.

WHY THIS ONE: no cookie consent banner needed (it doesn't track people
individually), loads fast, and the dashboard is dead simple — visitors,
top pages, where they came from. Perfect for "did FPRA people visit?"

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OPTION 2 — GOOGLE ANALYTICS  (free, more detailed, needs Google account)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Go to analytics.google.com, sign in with a Google account.
2. Create a property for everett-brothers.com.
3. It gives you a Measurement ID like  G-XXXXXXXXXX
4. In each HTML file, find the "OPTION 2: Google Analytics" comment,
   uncomment that block (remove <!-- and -->), and replace BOTH
   instances of G-XXXXXXXXXX with your real ID.

NOTE: Google Analytics uses cookies, so technically you should add a
cookie consent notice for EU/UK visitors. If most of your audience is
US, this matters less, but Plausible avoids the issue entirely.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHICH SHOULD YOU PICK?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For a clean personal/consulting site where you mainly want to know
"how many people visited and which pages" — go with a free privacy
option (Umami or Counter.dev) or Plausible. Skip Google Analytics
unless you specifically want its depth. Less setup, no cookie banner,
fits your minimal aesthetic.

After you paste it in and re-upload the files, visit your own site,
then check the dashboard — you should see your own visit appear.
